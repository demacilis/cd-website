1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

07.01.2020

There is a lot of factors that might impact a digital project. Someone might leave the project earlier, the company might be merging with another company with different go-to-market strategy, might be a lack of skills, recources, employees, budget might be exceede, etc. We can categorize them like this:

  • Known/Known – We are aware of these during planning and understand what to do should they occur.
  • Known/Unknown – We are aware of these during planning, but aren’t fully prepared to respond to them.
  • Unknown/Known – We aren’t aware of these, but know how to handle them if they occur.
  • Unknown/Unknown – We aren’t aware of these and don’t know how we would handle them.

    • For example Known/Known could be knowing that different browsers might display the content differently, so we have to make sure we’ll code it adequately.

    A different example might be when we know we might need to use databases (known) but we don’t know yet how to use them (unknown)

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

10.01.2020

One principal aim of GDPR is to increase protection for individuals’ personal data. It impacts digital projects on many levels restricting the usage of personal data:


Under GDPR, methods digital marketers use to obtain consent will be held to a much higher standard. Any marketing campaign using personal data procured in the past will need to meet them too. Any marketer using a tool such as Facebook Custom Audiences will need separate consent from the individual for their email address to be used for promoted social media posts.

Under GDPR, it will be easier for individuals to withdraw their consent for data processing. Also, under GDPR individuals have a right to access any data you hold on them too. This includes when, how and to what they consented, plus the data you hold.

Finally, digital marketers will only be able to process the data they really need, requiring legal justification for why the personal data they’ve collected is being processed.
